# Stage 1 Audit

## Scope completed

- final research problem specified;
- theoretical mechanism specified;
- research gap bounded;
- six research questions defined;
- six hypotheses mapped to variables;
- units of analysis separated;
- core constructs operationalized;
- observational identification limits stated;
- falsification criteria stated;
- foundational bibliography created;
- source-verification ledger created.

## Logic checks

### Check 1: Does the outcome match the evidence?

Pass. The design measures textual representation, disclosure, and market quality. It does not infer audience trust or behavior.

### Check 2: Is public sentiment confused with attention or online expression?

Pass. Google Trends, news tone, and social-media sentiment are explicitly rejected as gold standards for public opinion.

### Check 3: Is price treated as probability without assumptions?

Pass. Manski and Wolfers–Zitzewitz motivate a conditional interpretation.

### Check 4: Is market quality reduced to cumulative volume?

Pass. Quality is multidimensional; volume alone is prohibited.

### Check 5: Is representational overreach circular?

Conditional pass. The construct is decomposed into independently measured claim breadth, market quality, and disclosure. Stage 3 must verify that claim breadth and disclosure can be coded reliably. Stage 2 must verify timestamp-aligned quality data.

### Check 6: Are partnership effects described causally?

Pass. All partnership hypotheses are associational.

### Check 7: Can hypotheses be falsified?

Pass. Explicit thesis-weakening outcomes are specified.

## Feasibility risks for Stage 2

1. Historical order-book snapshots may be unavailable for all cited markets.
2. Exact broadcast timestamps may be difficult to establish.
3. Formal partnership classification requires dated documentation.
4. Polymarket and Kalshi measures may not be directly comparable.
5. Article full text may be copyrighted and unsuitable for repository distribution.

## Mitigation decisions

- retain source URLs and derived features; do not commit copyrighted full-text corpora unless licensing permits;
- prioritize Kalshi as the primary platform;
- separate confirmatory timestamp-matched markets from unmatched descriptive cases;
- standardize quality measures within platform;
- preserve a source inventory and hashes for reproducibility;
- document every unmatched article-market pair.

## Stage gate

**PASS WITH DATA-AUDIT CONDITIONS.**

The design is ready for Stage 2. It should not yet be described as fully executable until the following are confirmed:

- historical trade and quote coverage;
- practical article-retrieval coverage;
- sufficient number of matched market mentions;
- minimum sample size for source-type comparisons.

No theoretical redesign is required before the data audit.

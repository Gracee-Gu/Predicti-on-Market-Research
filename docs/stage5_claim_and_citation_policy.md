# Claim and Citation Policy

Every substantive manuscript claim must have one of four support types:

- `theory_source`: supported by cited scholarship;
- `design_record`: supported by a protocol, codebook, or preregistered rule;
- `empirical_output`: supported by a Stage 4 table, figure, model, or audit;
- `interpretive_inference`: explicitly labeled as interpretation and linked to supporting evidence.

The claim ledger records the manuscript section, claim text, support type, supporting path, claim status, and whether the claim is publication-ready.

The following are prohibited:

- claiming historical order-book quality from current snapshots;
- treating platform-owned media as representative of independent journalism;
- presenting AI-only adjudication as human adjudication;
- converting an exploratory association into a causal effect;
- omitting failed claim gates from the results narrative;
- committing copyrighted article text when hashes, metadata, and excerpts are sufficient.

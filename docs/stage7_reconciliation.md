# Reconciliation of the Original Plan and the Current Repository

## What changed

The original plan treated Stages 4 and 5 as the two main empirical contributions:

1. comparative framing analysis across platform-owned, partner, independent, and regulatory/critical sources;
2. market-quality analysis for markets cited by those sources.

The later repository workflow moved earlier than planned into claim gating, manuscript assembly, reproducibility packaging, and submission preparation.

That shift was partly justified: the pipeline correctly detected serious evidence limitations and did not silently fabricate market-quality results. However, packaging an exploratory single-source corpus as a nearly final paper would leave the original research question unanswered.

## Academic judgment

The project should not abandon the original research question merely because the first collection pass produced a narrow corpus. Instead, Stage 7 distinguishes:

- **design failure**: the intended evidence cannot reasonably be collected;
- **collection incompleteness**: the intended evidence is possible but has not yet been assembled;
- **analysis limitation**: evidence exists but supports only exploratory claims;
- **finalization readiness**: both empirical components are sufficiently implemented and audited.

## Current likely status

Based on the Stage 4–6 audit pattern already documented in the repository, the likely current status is `empirical_recovery_required`, because:

- the analyzed corpus is concentrated in platform-owned material;
- cross-source comparisons cannot answer the planned framing question;
- publication-aligned market-quality coverage is extremely low or absent;
- AI-assisted coding exists without sufficient documented human adjudication.

The Stage 7 scripts verify these conditions from the actual repository rather than hard-coding that conclusion.

## Stage 7 principle

The final deliverable must visibly show four-credit research effort while remaining academically honest. A rigorous gap audit and recovery workflow is preferable to a polished paper whose central empirical claims are unsupported.

# Stage 5 Manuscript Plan

## Purpose

The final manuscript connects three claims: prediction-market prices are manufactured public signals; media framing can detach those signals from market-quality conditions; and representational overreach occurs when market prices are described as public opinion, democratic judgment, or collective belief without appropriate caveats.

## Section logic

1. **Introduction:** establish the public-signals problem and contribution.
2. **Theory and hypotheses:** connect framing, market quality, and representational overreach.
3. **Data and methods:** describe corpus construction, article–market matching, coding provenance, reliability, market-quality alignment, and claim gates.
4. **Results:** reproduce only Stage 4 outputs and state failed gates alongside estimates.
5. **Discussion:** distinguish evidence about the observed corpus from general claims about media or prediction markets.
6. **Limitations:** source concentration, selection, matching uncertainty, missing market-quality data, AI-assisted coding, and observational identification.
7. **Reproducibility statement:** identify scripts, inputs, outputs, and excluded copyrighted material.

## Interpretation hierarchy

- `confirmatory_ready`: hypothesis language is permitted, but causal language remains prohibited without an identification design.
- `exploratory_only`: describe within-corpus associations and patterns.
- `blocked`: report descriptive diagnostics only.
